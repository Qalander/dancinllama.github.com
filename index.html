Midwest Dreamin Hackathon

The Salesforce 1 Lightning platform allows developers, administrators, and business analysts to build apps, integrate data and automate processes lightning fast.  Interested in learning more and exploring Lightning? Come hack with us at Midwest Dreamin' 2015! 


Hacking takes place June 29th - July 8th, 2015


The lightning fast hack starts on June 29th, 2015 at 8am CDT.  All  hacks must be submitted no later than 5pm CDT on July, 8th 2015. See below for additional rules:


  1. The project must utilize one or more of the following:


Lightning Connect 

   Lightning Process Builder

 Lightning Components

 Lightning App Builder

  1. The more aspects of lightning used, the better your score.
  2. Signup for your own Salesforce 1 Developer Org on or after June 29th.  
  3. Create a System Administrator user and assign it to mwdhack@gmail.com
  4. Go to <URL> and register your team.
  5. If any projects are started before June 29th, they will be disqualified.
  6. All work must be done by 5pm on July 8th, 2015.
  7. By 5pm CDT on July 8th, 2015, send an email to mwdhack@gmail.com and include a link to a video demonstration of your application.
  8. The video demonstration must be 10 minutes or less in duration.
  9. Around 8am CDT on July 9th, we will announce the 5 finalists for the Midwest Dreamin Hackathon, and unlock their user accounts within their Salesforce organization.
  10. The 5 finalists have until <4 pm>? on July 9th, 2015 to prepare for a live demo in front of the Midwest Dreamin' Hackathon judges.  
  11. The live demos will each be 10 minutes in length.
  12. The finalists must be present in the room in order to be eligible for winning the Midwest Dreamin' 2015 Hackathon.
  13. The judges will collaborate and the winner will be announced during the closing keynote at Midwest Dreamin' 2015 (<5 pm?>) on July 9th, 2015.

Judging Criteria

The following are criteria the judges will use to determine the Midwest Dreamin' 2015 Hackathon finalists and winner

  1. The amount of Lightning aspects used in the application
  2. Creativity
  3. Application of business use cases
  4. Usability of the application
